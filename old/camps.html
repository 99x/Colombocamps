Camps

The community has been constantly arranging many camps and have invited many international and local speakers to share experiences and knowledge for the betterment of the Sri Lankan ICT Industry. These have been highly interactive events which provided the attendees with hands-on experience on the topics.



Colombo SharePoint Camp

The Colombo SharePoint Camp was organized for any SharePoint geek to get a first class experience with a variety of SharePoint industry inside practices. It opened a door to listen to SharePoint gurus from international and local communities and get insights in to the latest trends, the SharePoint practices and its ecosystem. This was a fantastic opportunity for networking and also a chance for everyone to mingle with fellow SharePoint buddies for sharing experiences.

Official Website : www.colombosharepointcamp.com |

Colombo iPhone Dev Camp

The Colombo iPhone Dev Camp was the place for the local iOS Developers to "Develop, Connect and Master" with Apple Apps. This was the first of its kind in the country, where the participants could experience the creation of apps in a "LIVE" environment, getting a hands-on experience. Some of the local industry's best practitioners guided them in discovering new benchmarks with this brilliant platform.

Official Website : www.colomboiphonedevcamp.com |


Colombo Agile Camp

Code crammers come by the dozens from Asia. But building a distributed Agile team takes a lot more than good programmers. There are multiple barriers to be broken: time, communication, adapting business domain, and not the least - cultural and inter human relations. Agile methods require a totally different approach than the 'set and forget' approach of traditional offshore project management. Bridging these gaps and breaking these impediments are vital for successful offshore Agile development strategies.

Official Website : www.colomboagilecamp.com |

Colombo Code Camp

Initiated by dedicated professionals of the local IT industry, CCC was focused on improving technology competencies of Sri Lankan developers.The event brought together some of the best IT geeks in Sri Lanka in a highly interactive and the informative one day session provided a platform to demonstrate and develop latest natural user interface technologies facilitated by Ragnar Harper, one of the world's most renowned architects on Microsoft based technologies.
